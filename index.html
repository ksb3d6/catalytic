At the centre top, neoclouds and hyperscalers now sit in separate boxes, fed by three capital sources. The pay ribbons show where the money actually comes from: private credit (~43% of the funding bundle) pours almost entirely into neoclouds — debt-financed through GPU- and offtake-pledged SPVs — while hyperscalers self-fund via IG bonds and operating cash (the blue loop recycles it back) and take only a hair-thin ≈2% of private credit. Both buyers then fund the physical build-out — compute, power and networking — with hyperscalers carrying the larger share of power and the network (they build the data centres, substations and fibre themselves) and neoclouds the more compute-concentrated, debt-financed buyer. The spine then flows down — compute → memory → foundry → equipment & EDA — and steps off to raw materials. Networking silicon reconverges into the foundry from the side, so the fabs are the shared chokepoint — a web, not a tree. The fragility isn’t the blue-chip hyperscalers — it is the neocloud / private-credit junction. Bands and tethers are impressionistic, not measured.
